This week introduces the basic concepts of vector maths. Vectors are used to represent positions and distances in 2D and 3D space, vital for almost all games.

This week we also look at the various ways that data can be passed between different parts of a program. In particular, the notion of indirection through references or pointers is a powerful but often misunderstood idea.

Learning Objectives:
  • Recall key concepts of vector mathematics
  • Calculate with vectors and apply calculations to real problems
  • Explain the difference between passing by reference and passing by value
